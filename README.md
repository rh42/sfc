# A Gentle Tour of Structure–Function Coupling

A small explainer site about **structure–function coupling (SFC)**: why structural and functional connectivity mismatch, how communication models bridge that gap, and what SFC actually scores.

The site is organised into three sections:

1. **The mismatch** – structural wiring is sparse, functional connectivity is dense, and their zero-structures do not line up.
2. **How signals travel** – shortest paths, random walks, and communicability all take the same adjacency matrix and produce different predicted connectivity patterns.
3. **What SFC measures** – comparing those predictions to empirical functional connectivity to get a per-region coupling score, and mapping out the resulting gradient.

---

## What this is

- A visual, opinionated explainer of SFC for people who already know the basics of structural and functional connectivity.
- A way to make the phrase “structure–function coupling” point to something concrete rather than just another acronym.

## What this isn’t

- A full survey of the SFC literature or of all possible communication models.
- Evidence that SFC, by itself, “explains” cognition, intelligence, or behaviour.
